Karaage


Description

Experience the crispy, succulent delight of Karaage, a Japanese fried chicken that promises to tantalize your taste buds. Marinated in a flavorful blend of soy sauce, ginger, and garlic, each piece of chicken is fried to golden perfection, delivering a juicy and tender interior wrapped in a crunchy, savory crust. To elevate this delectable dish, it's paired with a Honey Mayoster Sauce, a delightful mix of creamy mayonnaise and sweet honey with a hint of tang. This sauce perfectly complements the savory chicken, adding a sweet and tangy twist that enhances each bite. Ideal as a snack, appetizer, or main course, Karaage with Honey Mayoster Sauce brings a delicious taste of Japan to your table, making it a favorite for both casual meals and special occasions.

Ingredients

  • 1 Â½ tablespoons low-sodium soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon honey
  • 2 teaspoons minced garlic
  • 2 teaspoons minced fresh ginger
  • 1 egg
  • 1 cup panko bread crumbs
  • vegetable oil for frying

Steps

  1. Mix 1 1/2 tablespoon soy sauce, 1 tablespoon honey, garlic, and ginger together in a bowl. Add chicken; turn to coat. Marinate in the refrigerator, 8 hours to overnight.
  2. Whisk egg in a bowl until smooth.
  3. Pour panko bread crumbs into a shallow bowl. Dip chicken pieces in whisked egg and roll in panko until coated.
  4. Heat oil in a deep-fryer or large saucepan to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Cook chicken in batches in the hot oil until golden brown, about 5 minutes. Transfer to paper towels to drain excess oil.
  5. Mix Japanese mayonnaise, 2 tablespoons honey, Dijon mustard, mirin, and 1 tablespoon soy sauce together to make sauce. Pour sauce over chicken.
